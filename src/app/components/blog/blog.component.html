When buying a plot or flat in Chennai, you often hear the terms DTCP approved and CMDA approved. Many buyers get confused about the difference. Understanding these approvals is essential to avoid legal issues later.

In this guide, TrustyPlots explains in simple words what DTCP and CMDA mean, and which approval is needed for your property.

What is CMDA Approval?

The Chennai Metropolitan Development Authority (CMDA) controls planning and development within the Chennai Metropolitan Area.

  • Covers Chennai Corporation and nearby suburbs in parts of Chengalpattu, Thiruvallur, and Kanchipuram.
  • If the property is within the metro region, new projects/layouts should have CMDA approval.
  • Examples: Anna Nagar, Tambaram, Sholinganallur, Ambattur, Porur, Velachery, Pallikaranai.

What is DTCP Approval?

The Directorate of Town and Country Planning (DTCP) grants approvals outside the CMDA jurisdiction.

  • Applies to municipalities, town panchayats, and rural areas.
  • Ensures legal conversion of agricultural land before selling as plots.
  • Examples around Chennai: Oragadam, Sriperumbudur, Guduvanchery, Villupuram, Cuddalore, and outer Kanchipuram/Chengalpattu areas.
